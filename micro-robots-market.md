<p><p>The micro-robots market is categorized into semi-autonomous and autonomous types. Semi-autonomous micro-robots require human oversight for navigation and task execution, often utilizing remote controls or pre-defined commands. In contrast, autonomous micro-robots operate independently, utilizing onboard sensors and algorithms to make real-time decisions without human intervention. These distinctions influence applications across industries, with semi-autonomous models being suited for tasks needing human expertise, while autonomous models excel in environments requiring minimal supervision, such as drug delivery and environmental monitoring.</p></p>
