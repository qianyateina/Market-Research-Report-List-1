Modified Hodge testing is a phenotypic method to detect carbapenemase activity, allowing for easy identification of resistant strains in clinical samples. Minimal Inhibitory Concentration (MIC) testing measures the lowest concentration of antibiotics needed to inhibit bacterial growth, providing crucial data on resistance levels. Other types encompass molecular and rapid diagnostic tests that leverage advanced technologies for quick, accurate identification and susceptibility profiling of CRE.</p></p>
