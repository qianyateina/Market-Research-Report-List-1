<p><p>The electric car battery market primarily consists of three types: lithium-ion (Li-ion) batteries, nickel-metal hydride (Ni-MH) batteries, and fuel cells. Lithium-ion batteries dominate due to their high energy density, longer life, and lightweight properties, making them ideal for electric vehicles. Ni-MH batteries are commonly found in hybrid vehicles, offering good performance but lower energy density compared to Li-ion. Fuel cells convert hydrogen into electricity, providing a clean energy alternative with quick refueling, though currently less widespread than battery technologies.</p></p>
